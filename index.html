HANGMAN

The Hangman game has dark origins in 17th- and 18th-century Europe. Criminals sentenced to death by hanging would sometimes get the opportunity to play a game called "Rite of Words and Life". The executioner would choose a word and the criminal would have to guess the word letter by letter before the blanks were filled. If one were to guess the correct answer, their life would be spared. Not solving the answer would seal the criminal's fate
